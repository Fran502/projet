La méthode Gendermag

« Definition: The GenderMag method is an analytical method for evaluating software
(i) according to the following five facets of gender differences: motivation, information processing style, computer self-efficacy, risk aversion and tinkering;
(ii) which are encapsulated into a set of faceted personas, each of which has a gender and has research-based facet values for all five facets;
(iii) using a gender-specialized [cognitive walkthrough] process that integrates references to the facets and to the selected persona throughout. »

Comme définit ci-dessus, le système d'évaluation Gendermag, dont trois personas détaillés sont une composante intégrale, vise d'encourager la création du logiciel qui se convient aux besoins et aux auto-perceptions de toutes utilisateurs. Burnett et al. expliquent bien pourquoi cette recherche porte sur la question de genre:

« In recent years, research into gender differences has established that individual differences in how people problem-solve often cluster by gender. Research also shows that these differences have direct implications for software that aims to support users’ problem-solving activities, and that much of this software is more supportive of problem-solving processes favored (statistically) more by males than by females »
